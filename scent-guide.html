How to Choose a Perfume

Finding a perfume that works for you takes a bit of trial and error. The same scent can smell completely different on two people. Here is how to find yours:

1. Understand Scent Families

Fragrances generally fall into these families:

  • Floral: Rose, jasmine, lavender. Romantic and timeless.
  • Oriental: Amber, vanilla, musk. Warm and sensual.
  • Fresh: Citrus, green notes, aquatic. Light and energising.
  • Woody: Sandalwood, cedar, oud. Grounded and earthy.

2. Consider the Occasion

Think about when and where you will wear it. Light fresh scents like Citrus Bloom work well for daytime and the office. Richer scents like Amber Nights suit evenings and special occasions. Velvet Rose sits somewhere in the middle — an everyday floral that works any time.

3. Test on Your Skin

Fragrances develop differently on everyone. The same perfume can smell different on your skin versus a friend's. Always test on your wrist and let it settle for a few minutes to experience the full journey from top to base notes.

How to Use a Reed Diffuser

Getting the most out of your Miss Vee home diffuser is simple:

  • Placement: Put your diffuser in a room with good airflow, away from direct sunlight and air conditioners.
  • Activation: Insert the rattan reeds into the bottle. Flip them after the first hour for immediate scent.
  • Maintenance: Flip the reeds every 1–2 weeks to refresh the scent intensity.
  • Lifespan: A 200ml diffuser lasts approximately 8–12 weeks.
  • Safety: Keep out of reach of children and pets. Avoid contact with polished surfaces.

Scent Families Explained

Floral Scents

Floral scents are the most traditional category in perfumery, built around real flower extracts. Our Velvet Rose is a personal favourite.

Warm & Spicy Scents

Warm, cosy, and a little bit addictive. Think amber, vanilla, cardamom, saffron. Amber Nights is exactly this — like a blanket you can wear.

Fresh Scents

Clean, bright, and energising. These are your citrus, green, and watery notes. Citrus Bloom is exactly what it sounds like — morning energy in a bottle.

Tips for Making Your Fragrance Last

  • Apply perfume to pulse points — wrists, neck, behind ears, and inside elbows.
  • Moisturise your skin before applying — fragrance lasts longer on hydrated skin.
  • Don't rub your wrists together — this breaks down the scent molecules.
  • Store perfumes in a cool, dark place away from sunlight.
  • Layer your scent — use the matching diffuser in your home to surround yourself.
